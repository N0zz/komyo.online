🎨 Graphics detail review — upgrade vs keep simple

Drag each divider. LEFT = the game's current look (reproduced). RIGHT = the proposed detail level (same style and palette, more material/light detail — matching Frog Bonk's bar, not its meadow style).

1 · Keep Defender (tower-defense) — verdict: UPGRADE, biggest win

Flattest board in the catalogue: checkerboard tiles + solid path. Pass: grass texture & patch variation, worn path with edges/stones, stone-built towers with AO and shadows, one light direction, grade+vignette.

2 · Meadow Flyer (flappy) — verdict: UPGRADE, big win

Same organic-world family as Frog Bonk. Pass: layered sky with depth haze, parallax hill tones, leafy stem-textured pipes with lit rims, a shaded bird with a real eye and wing, drifting petals, grade.

3 · Bubble Pop (bubbles) — verdict: UPGRADE, medium — gloss pass, not painterly

The candy theme wants glossy materials: dual-specular candy orbs with inner tint and drop shadows, subtle board depth glow. Readability of colours must stay king — no texture noise on the orbs.

4 · Stack (stacker) — verdict: LIGHT TOUCH — borderline, could also stay as-is

Its minimal pastel look is arguably the style. A light material pass (soft block gradients, rim light, contact shadows, ambient background depth) keeps the minimalism but adds physicality. Cheapest of the four.

Keep simple — flat/glow IS the identity (no detail pass)

  • Neon Snake — neon grid glow; texture would fight the aesthetic. (Polish lever if ever wanted: bloom/trail quality, not detail.)
  • Brick Breaker — synthwave = clean geometry + glow + horizon grid. Already style-complete.
  • Forcefield — dark space + neon energy arcs; its planet-glow look is intentional and readable.
  • Range (aim-trainer) — tactical readability is the point; any visual noise costs precision.
  • Asteroids / Asteroids+ — deliberate classic vector look; "upgrading" it would erase the identity.
